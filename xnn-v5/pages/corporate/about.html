About Xana National News

XNN is the leading global news organisation headquartered in the capital city of Xana. For over four decades we have delivered breaking news, investigative journalism and multimedia storytelling to millions of readers across six continents. Our commitment to independence, accuracy and public service journalism has made us a trusted voice in times of crisis and celebration.

Our History

Founded in 1982 as the state broadcaster’s news division, XNN began as a modest wire service serving government agencies. The collapse of the old regime in 1990 and the country’s subsequent embrace of democracy transformed our mission. We were re‑launched in 1992 as Xana National News, a public‑interest news organisation owned by a mixed public‑private trust. Over the next three decades we expanded our footprint with bureaus in New York, London, Nairobi, Tokyo, São Paulo and Sydney.

Key milestones:

  • 1992 – Rebranded as XNN with editorial independence enshrined in law.
  • 2001 – Launched first international bureau in London.
  • 2008 – Began digital transition; website attracted 10 million monthly users within a year.
  • 2015 – Expanded into broadcast with XNN-TV and XNN Radio.
  • 2023 – Introduced our strategic intelligence division and global investigative consortium.

Mission & Values

Our mission is to inform, engage and empower communities by delivering accurate, impartial and courageous journalism. We stand by the following core values:

  • Integrity: We report the truth, even when it is uncomfortable.
  • Independence: We operate free from political or commercial influence.
  • Excellence: We pursue the highest standards in reporting and storytelling.
  • Inclusivity: We amplify diverse voices and examine power structures.
  • Innovation: We embrace new technologies to reach audiences wherever they are.

Global Bureaus & Partnerships

With more than 80 bureaus around the world and partnerships with regional media organisations, XNN maintains a truly global presence. Our network is designed to provide on‑the‑ground coverage of events big and small, from conflict zones to community festivals.

Leadership

Under the guidance of our Board of Trustees, a team of experienced journalists and executives drives XNN forward. Key leaders include:

  • Maria Elena Torres – Chief Executive Officer, former foreign correspondent and bureau chief.
  • David Singh – Editor‑in‑Chief, oversees newsroom operations globally.
  • Professor Laila Hamid – Chair of the Board of Trustees, academic and media law specialist.

Awards & Recognition

Our investigative teams have won numerous international awards, including the Golden Press Freedom Prize (2010), three Pulitzer Prizes (2013, 2017, 2022) and the World Digital Media Award for Innovation (2024).
