Corrections Policy

Last updated: February 23, 2026

XNN is committed to accuracy. When we make a mistake in our reporting—in fact, dates, names, figures or any other factual information—we correct it promptly and transparently. Our Corrections Policy describes the procedures we follow to identify, verify and amend errors.

How Corrections Are Made

  • Errors reported by readers, sources or staff are logged in our corrections database.
  • Editorial staff review the claim, consult original sources and, if necessary, seek additional verification.
  • If an error is confirmed, we update the online article immediately and append a correction notice at the bottom of the story specifying what was changed and when.
  • For print versions, a correction appears in the next available issue page 2 on the "Corrections" column.

Types of Corrections

  • Correction: Fix of a factual error that does not alter the story’s overall meaning.
  • Clarification: Additional information provided where wording may have been ambiguous or misunderstood.
  • Retraction: Withdrawal of an article or section that contains significant errors undermining the entire piece.

Reporting an Error

If you believe we have published an inaccuracy, please contact our Corrections Desk at corrections@xnn.xana.gov or use the form on our Contact page. Include a link to the story and a description of the issue.

Our Commitment

We strive to build trust with our audience. Our newsroom fosters a culture where admitting and fixing errors is encouraged. Editors and reporters are required to review corrections quarterly and identify patterns to improve processes.
