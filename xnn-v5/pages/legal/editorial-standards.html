Editorial Standards

Last updated: February 23, 2026

XNN’s mission depends on public trust. Our Editorial Standards serve as a compass for journalists across the organization, articulating the principles that guide reporting, editing and presentation. All newsroom personnel must adhere to these standards.

Accuracy and Verification

  • Report only what can be verified through trusted sources or documents.
  • Use multiple independent sources for all significant claims, particularly those that could harm a person’s reputation.
  • Attribute information clearly, specifying who said what and under what conditions (e.g., on/off the record).
  • Distinguish clearly between fact, opinion and analysis. Opinions should be labeled as such.

Fairness and Impartiality

  • Avoid conflicts of interest and disclose any relevant relationships.
  • Give subjects a reasonable opportunity to respond to allegations before publication.
  • Strive for balanced coverage, especially on contentious issues. Seek diverse viewpoints.

Privacy

  • Respect privacy rights. Do not publish private information about individuals without a compelling public-interest justification.
  • Handle information about minors, victims of crime, and private citizens with extra care.

Corrections and Accountability

  • Admit and correct errors promptly according to the Corrections Policy.
  • Journalists should be accountable for their work. Editors will review content for compliance with these standards.

Use of Imagery and Multimedia

  • Identify the source of all photographs, video and audio. Do not manipulate images in a misleading way.
  • Obtain consent when possible, particularly for interviews and close‑up images of private individuals.

Social Media and Personal Conduct

  • Staff should maintain professional behaviour on social platforms. Personal posts should not compromise perceived impartiality.
  • Do not use personal accounts to break news that should be distributed through official XNN channels.

These standards are reviewed annually by the editorial board. Questions or concerns can be directed to the Editor‑in‑Chief or the Ethics Committee.
